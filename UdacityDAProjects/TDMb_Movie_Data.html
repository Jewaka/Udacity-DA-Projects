Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

Background¶

TMDB (The Movie Database) is a community built movie and TV database. It has a strong international focus with about 39 languages it officially supports. It also has an extensive regional data with daily usage of the database in over 180 countries. Therefore, this a great place to hit the ground running in analysis of the dataset from this database.

TMDb movie data set contains information about 5,000 movies collected from The Movie Database (TMDb), including user ratings and revenue. The column names in the table are as follows:

  • budget
  • genres
  • homepage
  • id
  • keywords
  • original_language
  • original_title
  • overview
  • popularity
  • production_companies
  • production_countries
  • release_date
  • revenue
  • runtime
  • spoken_languages
  • status
  • tagline
  • vote_average
  • vote_count

Question(s) for Analysis¶

  1. Which genres are most popular from year to year?
  2. What kind of properties are associated with movies that have high revenues?

def correlation_coeff(x,y):
    std_x=(x-x.mean())/x.std(ddof=0)
    std_y=(y-y.mean())/y.std(ddof=0)
    return(std_x*std_y).mean()

Conclusions¶

In this project, we analyzed TMDb movie dataset for popular genres over the years and the properties associated with high revenue generating movies. The main insights are as follows:

  • In getting the counts of possible unique genres, Drama and Comedy had the highest total counts while in visualization with the number of years, Animation maintained highest popularity over a longer period compared to Drama or Comedy.
  • The top movies (Avatar, Titanic, The Avengers, Jurassic World etc) with highest revenues belonged to more than one genre.
  • Paramount Pictures and Universal Pictures production companies generate high revenues in movie production. Being in top 5.
  • Production companies has an effect on revenues of movies produced.
  • There is also a positive correlation between budget and movie revenues.

Limitation¶

  • More movies had multiple genres and in separating the genres, many rows were lost that contained NaN vlaues.
  • Getting a visual to establish association of production companies and revenue.
